# Cross-Channel Ads Dashboard Story

## Executive Summary

The dashboard shows a media mix where TikTok drives scale, Google drives measurable revenue, and Facebook contributes the most efficient non-revenue-tracked conversions. The current budget is heavily weighted toward TikTok, which generates the largest reach and conversion volume, but its CPA is higher than Facebook and Google. Google is the strongest revenue channel because it is the only source with conversion value data and delivers $210.9K in tracked revenue from $37.7K in spend.

The main business conclusion is that the account should not optimize only for conversion volume. Budget decisions should combine scale, CPA, and revenue tracking quality. TikTok is useful for reach and upper/mid-funnel volume, Google should be protected and expanded where ROAS is strong, and Facebook retargeting should be scaled carefully because it has strong conversion efficiency.

## Data Scope

- Period: January 1-30, 2024.
- Platforms: Facebook, Google, TikTok.
- Total spend: $130.2K.
- Total impressions: 40.5M.
- Total clicks: 688.3K.
- Total conversions: 13.4K.
- Total tracked revenue: $210.9K.
- Important caveat: revenue is available only for Google, so ROAS is not comparable across all platforms.

## Main Insights

### 1. TikTok Owns Scale, But Not Efficiency

TikTok accounts for 57.0% of spend, 70.9% of impressions, 67.1% of clicks, and 50.5% of conversions. This means TikTok is the main scale channel in the mix.

However, TikTok CPA is $11.00, worse than Google at $8.93 and Facebook at $7.64. TikTok also has the lowest conversion rate at 1.46%, compared with Google at 3.07% and Facebook at 2.69%.

Conclusion: TikTok is effective for volume and awareness, but budget increases should be tied to campaign-level CPA improvement, not only reach or clicks.

### 2. Google Is the Revenue Engine

Google spends $37.7K and produces all tracked revenue: $210.9K. Its blended ROAS is 5.60x.

The best Google campaigns are:

- Search Brand Terms: 9.81x ROAS, $5.10 CPA.
- Shopping All Products: 7.89x ROAS, $6.34 CPA.
- Display Remarketing: 5.15x ROAS, $9.72 CPA.
- Search Generic Terms: 2.02x ROAS, $24.80 CPA.

Conclusion: Google budget should be protected for brand search, shopping, and remarketing. Generic search needs tighter keyword, query, and bid optimization before scaling.

### 3. Facebook Has Strong Conversion Efficiency

Facebook has the smallest spend share at 14.0%, but the best platform CPA at $7.64. Its strongest campaign is Conversions Retargeting with $5.95 CPA and 6.26% conversion rate.

Traffic Drive Jan also performs well with $7.52 CPA. Video Views Campaign is weaker for direct-response outcomes, with $14.96 CPA and low CTR.

Conclusion: Facebook retargeting and traffic campaigns deserve incremental testing budget. Facebook video should be evaluated as an awareness tactic, not a conversion driver.

### 4. Campaign Efficiency Varies More Than Platform Efficiency

The campaign-level table shows that platform averages hide important differences. For example, TikTok Influencer Collab has high spend and high conversion volume, but Facebook Conversions Retargeting and Google Search Brand Terms are materially more efficient by CPA.

High-priority campaigns to keep or expand:

- Google Search Brand Terms.
- Google Shopping All Products.
- Facebook Conversions Retargeting.
- Facebook Traffic Drive Jan.

Campaigns to review before adding budget:

- Google Search Generic Terms.
- TikTok Traffic Campaign.
- Facebook Video Views Campaign.

Conclusion: the next optimization should happen at campaign level, not only platform level.

### 5. Momentum Increased in the Second Half of January

Spend increased from $56.2K in January 1-15 to $74.0K in January 16-30. Conversions also increased from 5.8K to 7.6K. CPA stayed nearly flat, moving from $9.77 to $9.73.

This suggests that the account scaled in the second half of the month without materially damaging conversion efficiency. However, blended ROAS decreased from 1.70x to 1.56x because tracked revenue did not grow at the same pace as spend.

Conclusion: scaling worked for conversion volume, but revenue quality should be monitored more closely before further budget increases.

## Recommended Improvements

### Budget Allocation

- Increase budget gradually on Google Search Brand Terms, Google Shopping, and Facebook Conversions Retargeting.
- Keep TikTok active for scale, but cap or reallocate spend from campaigns with CPA above the account average.
- Reduce or restructure Google Search Generic Terms until CPA improves from $24.80.

### Measurement

- Add comparable revenue or conversion value tracking for Facebook and TikTok.
- Separate conversion types if possible, such as lead, purchase, signup, and view-through conversion.
- Track assisted conversions for TikTok and Facebook to avoid undervaluing awareness and video campaigns.

### Campaign Optimization

- Split TikTok reporting into awareness, traffic, and conversion objectives.
- Use CPA and conversion rate as primary decision metrics for direct-response campaigns.
- Use video views, views per dollar, and engagement metrics only for awareness campaigns.

### Dashboard Improvements

- Add a revenue coverage warning when non-Google platforms have missing revenue.
- Add a campaign objective filter if objective data becomes available.
- Add weekly comparison cards to show whether spend, CPA, and ROAS are improving.
- Add a recommendation section that flags campaigns with high spend and weak CPA automatically.

## Final Conclusion

The current media mix is directionally healthy because spend scaled in January while CPA stayed stable. The strongest business opportunity is to rebalance from pure volume toward profitable and efficient conversion sources. Google should remain the primary revenue-tracked channel, Facebook retargeting should receive controlled incremental budget, and TikTok should be managed as a scale channel with stricter CPA guardrails.
